Drug Administration Routes

Route Description
Oral Medication is taken by mouth, usually in the form of tablets, capsules, or liquids.
Intravenous (IV) Medication is administered directly into a vein through an intravenous line.
Intradermal (ID) Medication is injected into the dermis layer of the skin.
Subcutaneous (SubQ or SC) Medication is injected into the fatty tissue layer just below the skin.
